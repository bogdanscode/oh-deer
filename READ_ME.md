# Oh Deer

## Background
My girlfriend's grandparents own a large backyard where they grow roses, snowball trees, and many other plants. As summer begins, deer are coming out to eat the plants. They visit mainly at night, when everyone is asleep, and they especially like to eat the roses.

## Current Condition
The deer have eaten multiple roses and continue to come at night to eat more. My girlfriend's grandparents are considering using a "liquid fence," also known as coyote urine, to deter them.

## Goal
The goal is to build a device that will scare away deer before they can eat the roses. The device should also look attractive so the grandparents will appreciate it in their garden.

## Analysis
Deer come to the garden at night when the area is quiet and the roses are easy to reach. The problem is not just that they are hungry, but that the garden is attractive to them and the current protection is limited. A good solution should work at the right time, in the right place, and still fit the garden's appearance.

## Countermeasures
Possible solutions include a motion-activated scare device, a motion sensor with lights or sound, a visual deterrent that makes the garden less inviting, and a scent-based deterrent such as coyote urine.

## Action Plan
The plan is to build a device that can detect whether a deer is nearby. Once it detects a deer, it will activate a light to scare it away, use a motion sensor to confirm movement, and record the reaction with a camera for feedback.

## Follow-up
The design will be tested after installation and adjusted if needed.
